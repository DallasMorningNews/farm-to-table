The ultimate
local summer meal

Chef Graham Dodds creates a feast with fresh-from-the-farm produce

Tina Danze | Special Contributor

Published June 20, 2016

    F

    arm-to-table. The phrase has been around so long that it’s on the verge of cliché. Yet the practice has only recently drawn a wider circle of home cooks around the core of early adopters. Greater availability of locally raised products has dovetailed with increased public awareness about where our food comes from and how it’s farmed.

    Early on, chefs were privileged figures in the farm-to-table movement, sourcing local products that shoppers couldn’t get. Although that’s still the case with specialty vegetables, North Texas home cooks have more options than ever for buying a cornucopia of farm-fresh goods. Farmers markets have multiplied across north Texas in the past few years; co-ops and CSAs — community supported agriculture groups — are also on the rise; and supermarkets have stepped up their game with more produce from Texas farms. It all adds up to bringing more mainstream cooks into the farm-to-table fold.

    We asked Graham Dodds, chef-owner of Wayward Sons, to share a summer menu that showcases locally sourced ingredients. Long before opening Wayward Sons, Dodds was an early supporter of local farmers and producers during his tenure as executive chef at Bolsa, and later at Hibiscus. His menu features a zesty, marinated grilled chicken dish; a dazzling ratatouille; an updated carrot salad with local goat cheese and honey; and a peach and berry cobbler that screams Texas summer.

    The local shopping list for Dodds’ menu includes Windy Meadows Chicken — which is famously noted on menus at many top Dallas restaurants. Like chefs, you can have the chicken delivered to your door now — or buy it at many Dallas area markets.

    Of course, you can’t source every ingredient in Dodds’ recipes locally. But you can find enough of them to put a local stamp on the dishes and enjoy a quality and freshness upgrade. Read on for Dodds’ recipes and the local sources behind them — plus additional sources that we tapped, including some for bread and wine.

    Summer Ratatouille

    Origins
    Recipe

    Squash and eggplant

    Comeback Creek Farm | Co Rd 2119, Pittsburg, TX 75686

    Dodds is among the chefs supporting this farm, but home cooks can too. You can buy a variety of seasonal vegetables through the farm’s Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) program (sign up at comebackcreek.com). Farm-share memberships are available for the year (summer, fall and spring), or just one season; choose your pick-up site: Deep Ellum, Richardson or Coppell.

    Tomatoes

    J.T. Lemley’s Tomatoes | 590 VZ County Rd 2143, Canton, TX 75103

    We like the tomatoes that this popular Canton farmer sells at the Dallas Farmers Market on Saturdays, in a stall on the north side of the shed.

    Herbs

    Herbco Texas, previously known as Generation Farms | 1109 N McKinney St, Rice, TX 75155 | herbco.net

    This certified organic herb farm supplies herbs to many Dallas-area supermarkets, sometimes under the store’s private label.

    Jerry's Grilled Chicken

    Origins
    Recipe

    Chicken

    Windy Meadows Chicken | Windy Meadows Chicken, Campbell, Texas

    Windy Meadows Family Farm raises all-natural chickens, bred on-site. The chickens are hatched in a cage-free environment and raised as free-range chickens in a pastured environment, which unlike commercial chicken farms, means lots of sun and fresh air. The chicken enjoys a great reputation among the local chef community.

    Carrot Salad with Shishito Peppers, Honey and Goat Cheese

    Origins
    Recipe

    Honey

    Texas Honeybee Guild Honey | Blackland Prairie, Texas

    This organization (txbeeguild.org) owns and operates over 200 honeybee colonies throughout North Texas’ Blackland Prairie. In addition to the Blackland Prairie honey, the guild also makes hyper-local ZIP code honey, sourced mostly from Dallas neighborhoods. The honey is sold at Dallas Farmers Market, Good Local Markets and Central Market.

    Goat Cheese

    Latte Da Dairy | 1304 Bridle Bit Rd, Flower Mound, TX 75022

    Latte Da Dairy (lattedadairy.com) raises Nubian and LaMancha goats on its farm to produce a variety of artisan goat milk cheeses. The dairy farm offers an open house in the fall for those who want to visit. The cheese is sold at Central Market, Scardello Artisan Cheese (Farmers Market location), Coppell Farmers Market, and Good Local Market (Lakeside location).

    Thumbelina/globe carrots, burgundy carrots and kale

    Tassione Farms | 807 County Road 466, Stephenville, TX

    Beloved by chefs, Rocky Tassione provides restaurants with hard-to-find specialty produce, year-round. Since everything that he grows is dedicated to restaurant chefs, we bought organic kale and carrots from Whole Foods Market to use in Dodds’ salad recipe.

    Peach and Berry Cobbler

    Origins
    Recipe

    Blueberries and blackberries

    Blueberry Basket | 12462 FM 315, Larue, TX 75770 | 903-677-3448

    This East Texas farm sells organic blueberries and blackberries to many local stores and markets, and welcomes visitors to come and pick their own berries. Weather permitting, fresh picked berries are sold at Market Street, Central Market and Green Grocer on Greenville, as well as the following farmers markets: Dallas Farmers Market; Good Local Market, Lakeside; Coppel Farmers Market; Frisco Farmers Market; and McKinney Farmers Market.

    Peaches

    Larken Farms Orchard | 3653 Greathouse Rd, Waxahachie, TX 75167 | larkenfarms.com

    This farm has over 10,000 trees with 30 varieties of peaches, which are sold at Coppell Farmers Market, Good Local Markets and Central Market. The farm also lets you pick your own.

    Cooper Farms | 301 I-45 Exit 198, Fairfield, TX 75840 | cooperpeaches.com

    Cooper Farms peaches are sold at Central Market and at the farm’s store on I-45 in Fairfield, if you happen to be driving by

    Meyer Lemon Rosemary Bread

    Village Baking Company | 5531 E University Blvd, Dallas, TX 75206 | villagebakingco.com

    This is one of our favorites. It’s especially good brushed with olive oil and grilled. If you want to be decadent, spread it with some Latte Da goat cheese.

    Kiepersol Estate Texas Syrah, 2012

    Kiepersol | 3933 FM 344 E, Tyler, TX 75703 | kiepersol.com

    This medium bodied wine’s jammy fruit and soft tannins make it a good fit for the spicy chicken and the tomato-rich ratatouille. It has just enough body to stand up to the chicken but doesn’t overwhelm it.
