Farm to your table

Chef Graham Dodds shares a menu that celebrates summer with the bounty of North Texas producers

    Farm to your table

    Chef Graham Dodds shares a menu that celebrates summer with the bounty of North Texas producers

    Tina Danze | Special Contributor

    Published June 20, 2016

    F

    arm-to-table. The phrase has been around so long that it’s on the verge of cliché. Yet the practice has only recently drawn a wider circle of home cooks around the core of early adopters. Greater availability of locally raised products has dovetailed with increased public awareness about where our food comes from and how it’s farmed.

    Early on, chefs were privileged figures in the farm-to-table movement, sourcing local products that shoppers couldn’t get. Although that’s still the case with specialty vegetables, North Texas home cooks have more options than ever for buying a cornucopia of farm-fresh goods. Farmers markets have multiplied across North Texas in the past few years; co-ops and CSAs — community-supported agriculture groups — are also on the rise; and supermarkets have stepped up their game with more produce from Texas farms. It all adds up to bringing more mainstream cooks into the farm-to-table fold.

    Graham Dodds, chef at Wayward Sons

    We asked Graham Dodds, chef-owner of Wayward Sons, to share a summer menu that showcases locally sourced ingredients. Long before opening Wayward Sons, Dodds was an early supporter of local farmers and producers during his tenure as executive chef at Bolsa, and later at Hibiscus. His menu features a zesty, marinated grilled chicken dish; a dazzling ratatouille; an updated carrot salad with local goat cheese and honey; and a peach and berry cobbler that screams Texas summer.

    The local shopping list for Dodds’ menu includes Windy Meadows Chicken — which is famously noted on menus at many top Dallas restaurants. Like chefs, you can have the chicken delivered to your door now — or buy it at many Dallas area markets.

    Of course, you can’t source every ingredient in Dodds’ recipes locally. But you can find enough of them to put a local stamp on the dishes and enjoy a quality and freshness upgrade. Read on for Dodds’ recipes and the local sources behind them — plus additional Texas sources that we tapped, including some for bread and wine.

    Carrot salad with peppers, honey and goat cheese

    {{ img.jpg('carrot-mobile','Carrot Salad', class='imagesMobile', sizes='(min-width: 320px) 500px, 90vw' ) }}
    Origins
    Recipe

    Honey

    Texas Honeybee Guild Honey
    Blackland Prairie
    txbeeguild.org

    This organization owns and operates over 200 honeybee colonies throughout North Texas’ Blackland Prairie. In addition to the Blackland Prairie honey, the guild also makes hyper-local ZIP code honey, sourced mostly from Dallas neighborhoods. The honey is sold at Dallas Farmers Market, Good Local Markets and Central Market.

    Goat Cheese

    Latte Da Dairy
    1304 Bridle Bit Rd, Flower Mound
    lattedadairy.com

    Latte Da Dairy raises Nubian and LaMancha goats on its farm to produce a variety of artisan goat milk cheeses. The dairy farm offers an open house in the fall for those who want to visit. The cheese is sold at Central Market, Scardello Artisan Cheese (Farmers Market location), Coppell Farmers Market, and Good Local Market (Lakeside location).

    Thumbelina/globe carrots, burgundy carrots and kale

    Tassione Farms
    807 Erath County Road 466, Stephenville

    Beloved by chefs, Rocky Tassione provides restaurants with hard-to-find specialty produce, year-round. Since everything that he grows is dedicated to restaurant chefs, we bought organic kale and carrots from Whole Foods Market to use in Dodds’ salad recipe.

    This salad is on the menu at Wayward Sons. Dodds says it was inspired by a carrot salad that Sharon Hage used to make at York Street and for events where the two collaborated. His addition of shishito peppers and kale makes a nice spicy and bitter counterpoint to the honey-sweetened goat cheese.

    Ingredients

    24 Thumbelina (also called globe) carrots (or substitute 18-20 baby carrots), washed well and quartered (peeling is optional)
    4 medium carrots (burgundy ones, if possible), peeled
    1/4 cup currants
    1/2 cup honey, preferably Texas Honey Bee Guild Honey (sold at Central Market), divided use
    1/4 cup orange juice
    12 ounces goat cheese, preferably Latte Da brand
    Salt and black pepper as needed
    2 tablespoons grapeseed oil, as needed
    12 shishito peppers, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
    1 bunch dinosaur kale leaves (also called cavolo nero or lacinato kale), stripped from the stalks and torn into bite-size pieces (or sliced 1/2-inch thick)
    Champagne Vinaigrette (recipe follows)

    Directions

    Place Thumbelina carrot quarters in a large pot, cover with cold water, and salt to taste. Bring to a simmer and cook until carrots are just tender when pierced with a sharp knife — about 1 to 2 minutes, depending on size. Drain and plunge into ice bath.

    Use a mandoline or a very sharp knife to slice remaining carrots as thinly as possible into rounds or ovals. Place carrot slices in a bowl of ice water to crisp for 30 minutes.

    Remove both cooked and raw carrots from water and dry on a towel. (May be prepared ahead of time, refrigerated.)

    In another pot, combine currants, 1/4 cup of the honey and the orange juice and bring to a simmer. Remove from heat and let sit for 30 minutes. Strain off any unabsorbed liquid and reserve for another use. Set currants aside (may be prepared a day ahead of time).

    Using a mixer, combine goat cheese with remaining 1/4 cup of honey. Season with salt and black pepper to taste (may be prepared a day ahead of time).

    Heat a large sauté pan over medium-high heat. Coat bottom with grapeseed oil. Add shishito peppers and toss until blistered. Add kale and toss until wilted, no more than 1 minute. Put peppers and kale into bowl with carrots and plumped currants. Add champagne vinaigrette, to taste. Season with salt and black pepper. Spread goat cheese on a large serving platter in a smooth layer and place carrot mixture on top. Serve immediately.

    Champagne Vinaigrette: In a bowl, whisk together 1 1/2 tablespoons champagne vinegar and 4 to 4 1/2 tablespoons olive oil, to taste. Add salt, to taste.

    Makes 6 to 8 servings.

    Jerry's grilled chicken

    {{ img.jpg('chicken-mobile','Jerrys grilled chicken', class='imagesMobile', sizes='(min-width: 320px) 500px, 90vw' ) }}
    Origins
    Recipe

    Chicken

    Windy Meadows Chicken
    8045 Hunt County Road 4209, Campbell

    Windy Meadows Family Farm raises all-natural chickens, bred on-site. The chickens are hatched in a cage-free environment and raised as free-range chickens in a pastured environment, which unlike commercial chicken farms, means lots of sun and fresh air. The chicken enjoys a great reputation among the local chef community.

    Windy Meadows chicken is sold at many stores, and is now available for home delivery at windymeadowsdelivered.com. Prices start at $5.49 per pound for a whole chicken. You can buy the chicken at Green Grocer on Greenville Avenue; Matador Meats in Plano; Local Yocal and Patina Green in McKinney; It’s Organic 2 U in Southlake; Earthwise Gardens in Denton; Farm to Fork in Arlington; Get Healthy in Rockwall; Hillbilly Nutrition Cooperative in Carrollton; and Double Oak Co-op in Flower Mound.

    Dodds gives credit for this chicken recipe to one of his line cooks at Hibiscus, who occasionally made it for the restaurant staff’s meal before service. “It was always crazy delicious. We called it magic chicken because it is so good, so juicy, and the spice rub so delicious, that everyone would be in a really good mood after they ate it,” Dodds says.

    Ingredients

    2 whole Windy Meadows chickens, cut into parts; or 4 bone-in breast halves and 4 leg and thigh quarters, also from Windy Meadows
    1/4 cup granulated onion
    1/4 cup granulated garlic
    1/4 cup black pepper, coarsely ground
    1/8 cup crushed red pepper flakes
    1/8 cup kosher salt
    1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
    1/4 cup chopped fresh rosemary
    2 lemons
    2 limes
    2 oranges

    Directions

    Place chicken parts in a large, nonreactive bowl. In a separate bowl, combine the granulated onion, granulated garlic, black and red peppers, salt, parsley and rosemary. Rub the chicken evenly with the spice mixture. Squeeze juice from the lemons, limes and oranges into a small bowl to combine; pour juice evenly over chicken to coat it. Cover and marinate overnight in the refrigerator.

    Pre-heat grill to medium-hot. Brush the grill grate clean. Using tongs, oil the grill grate with a wad of paper towels dipped in vegetable oil. Grill chicken, skin-side down, for about 10 minutes (watch for flare-ups). Turn chicken to the other side and cook for another 10 minutes (8 to 10 minutes for breasts).

    If breasts are done, remove them and keep warm. If not, move them to a cool part of the grill and continue cooking until done. Move leg and thigh quarters to cooler part of the grill. Cover grill and cook, turning once, until done (about 20 to 25 more minutes); the chicken is done when the juices run clear as the tip of a skewer or a sharp knife is inserted in the thickest part of a thigh. (If you are cooking over coals, they may burn down before the chicken is done; if you don’t want to replenish them, you can finish cooking the chicken in a 350 F oven.)

    Transfer chicken to a serving platter and serve immediately.

    Makes 8 to 10 servings.

    Summer ratatouille

    {{ img.jpg('rata-mobile','Summer ratatouille', class='imagesMobile', sizes='(min-width: 320px) 500px, 90vw' ) }}
    Origins
    Recipe

    Squash and eggplant

    Comeback Creek Farm
    Camp County Road 2119, Pittsburg

    Dodds is among the chefs supporting this farm, but home cooks can too. You can buy a variety of seasonal vegetables through the farm’s CSA program (sign up at comebackcreek.com). Farm-share memberships are available for the year (summer, fall and spring), or just one season; choose your pickup site: Deep Ellum, Richardson or Coppell.

    Tomatoes

    J.T. Lemley’s Tomatoes
    590 Van Zandt County Road 2143, Canton

    We like the tomatoes that this popular Canton farmer sells at the Dallas Farmers Market on Saturdays, in a stall on the north side of the shed.

    Herbs

    Herbco Texas, previously known as Generation Farms
    1109 N. McKinney St., Rice
    txbeeguild.org

    This certified organic herb farm supplies herbs to many Dallas-area supermarkets, sometimes under the store’s private label.

    This seasonal special at Wayward Sons is a versatile side dish — it pairs well with poultry, fish and meats. Dodds says it can also be served as a meatless entrée, topped with crumbled ricotta salata; splurge on a crusty loaf of artisan bread to go with it.

    Ingredients

    1 eggplant, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
    Kosher salt, or sea salt, to taste
    4 red bell peppers, cut in half, seeds removed
    5 tablespoons grapeseed oil, plus more as needed (divided use)
    1 medium onion, diced
    2 large cloves garlic, minced
    5 tomatoes, blanched, peeled and diced
    2 yellow summer squash, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
    2 zucchini, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
    1/2 teaspoon crushed chili flakes
    1 tablespoon sherry vinegar or more, to taste
    1 bunch chives, thinly sliced
    1/2 bunch Italian parsley, chopped
    1/2 to 3/4 bunch basil, chopped

    Directions

    Preheat oven to 400 F.

    Toss eggplant with salt to coat and let drain in a colander for at least 30 minutes.

    Toss halved peppers in bowl with just enough grapeseed oil to coat (about 1 tablespoon). Season with salt to taste. Place on a sheet pan and roast in oven for 20 to 30 minutes, or until soft. Remove from oven, let sit in a bowl covered with plastic wrap for 10 minutes (to steam the skins loose). Peel off skin, and cut into large dice.

    In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of the grapeseed oil over medium heat. Add onions, sprinkle with salt, and sweat onions (sauté without browning) until translucent; if you want, you can cover the pan as they sweat, but stir them frequently. Add minced garlic and tomatoes. Cook until juices are released from the tomatoes and the mixture thickens, about 20 to 30 minutes. Add diced roasted peppers and remove from heat (may be prepared a day ahead of time and refrigerated).

    In a large sauté pan, heat 3 tablespoons grapeseed oil over high heat. Working in batches, add summer squash; try not to overcrowd the pan, as it prevents caramelization. Sprinkle liberally with salt. Do not shake pan, but allow single layer of squash to brown well on one side. Once browned, stir and sauté, for a minute or two to lightly cook the other sides. Remove from pan and repeat process with the zucchini and then the eggplant, still working in batches. Combine seared vegetables in the pot with the tomatoes and peppers. Bring to a simmer. Add the chili flakes, vinegar, chives, parsley and basil. Cook just until vegetables are cooked through — not too long, or they will begin to fall apart. Adjust the seasonings to taste. Serve warm.

    Makes 8 servings.

    SOURCE: Graham Dodds

    Peach and berry cobbler

    {{ img.jpg('cobbler-mobile','Peach and berry cobbler', class='imagesMobile', sizes='(min-width: 320px) 500px, 90vw' ) }}
    Origins
    Recipe

    Blueberries and blackberries

    Blueberry Basket
    12462 FM315, Larue

    This East Texas farm sells organic blueberries and blackberries to many local stores and markets, and welcomes visitors to come and pick their own berries. Weather permitting, fresh-picked berries are sold at Market Street, Central Market and Green Grocer on Greenville, as well as Dallas Farmers Market; Good Local Market, Lakeside; Coppell Farmers Market; Frisco Farmers Market; and McKinney Farmers Market.

    Peaches

    Larken Farms Orchard
    3653 Greathouse Road, Waxahachie
    larkenfarms.com

    This farm has over 10,000 trees with 30 varieties of peaches, which are sold at Coppell Farmers Market, Good Local Markets and Central Market. The farm also lets you pick your own.


    Cooper Farms
    301 I-45 Exit 198, Fairfield

    These peaches are sold at Central Market and at the farm’s store on I-45 in Fairfield, if you happen to be driving by.

    This cobbler is another seasonal special at Wayward Sons. Dodds serves it with a local vanilla ice cream that is not sold in stores. You can serve it with Henry’s Homemade Ice Cream for a local option, or with whipped cream.

    Ingredients

    For crust:
    12 ounces all-purpose flour (about 2 2/3 cups)
    1 teaspoon salt
    8 ounces (2 sticks) chilled butter, cut into cubes
    3 ounces ice water
    1 egg yolk
    1/4 cup half and half
    Turbinado sugar, for sprinkling

    For filling:
    6 to 7 cups diced ripe peaches
    3 cups blackberries or blueberries, or a combination of the two
    3/4 cup flour
    4 teaspoons cinnamon
    1 teaspoon salt
    6 tablespoons butter, cubed
    1/2 cup brown sugar

    Directions

    Prepare crust dough:

    Place flour, salt and butter in a food processor. Pulse to combine (it will resemble coarse meal). Add ice water and process just until mixture comes together to form a dough. Turn dough out onto a floured board and knead until it holds together. Wrap in plastic wrap and chill for 30 minutes to an hour.


    To make filling and assemble cobbler:

    Preheat oven to 350 F. Butter a 9x12-inch baking dish (or other 3-quart casserole dish that’s about 2 inches deep).

    Gently combine peaches, blackberries, flour, cinnamon, salt, butter and brown sugar in a bowl. Place mixture in prepared casserole dish. Set aside.

    Roll out crust dough on a floured surface to 1/4-inch thickness. Lay crust on top of casserole pan and trim edges. Combine the egg yolk and half and half; brush the crust with this mixture. Sprinkle crust very liberally with turbinado sugar.

    Place baking dish on a rimmed baking sheet (to catch any juices that bubble over when baking). Bake for 50 to 60 minutes, or until crust is golden brown and filling is bubbly. If desired, serve with whipped cream or ice cream.

    Makes 8 servings.
